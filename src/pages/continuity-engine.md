# Continuity Engine

The Continuity Engine is how an EverArcade world remembers itself.

Instead of treating each session as temporary, EverArcade focuses on living world history: what changed, who changed it, and how the world should continue from there.

## What continuity feels like

Continuity is visible in the world:

- Roads emerge because travelers repeatedly choose the same path.
- Forests spread when left alone and recede when harvested.
- Cultures form around rituals, victories, failures, and shared places.
- Economies evolve as players discover supply chains and trusted roles.
- Buildings age, become restored, or turn into ruins with stories attached.

## Why it matters

Players invest more when a place remembers them. Developers can build deeper systems when history is a foundation. Operators can help preserve a world because continuity is portable and verifiable.

## From technical proof to public trust

EverArcade documentation still explains the implementation details. The public promise is simpler: world history can be independently checked, recovered, and continued.
