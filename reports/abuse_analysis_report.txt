Abuse Analysis Report
Date: 2026-06-05
Result: HIGH RISK UNTIL SCAFFOLD BOUNDARIES ARE ENFORCED

Covered abuse classes
- economic abuse
- marketplace abuse
- GPU provider fraud
- fake render artifacts
- settlement spoofing
- Xaman payload spoofing
- replay divergence
- checkpoint poisoning
- malicious game templates
- creator marketplace spam
- player identity abuse
- operator misbehavior
- denial of service
- supply-chain risks
- open-source exploitation risks

Top findings
1. Deterministic records can be forged or misrepresented as production truth.
2. Revenue and marketplace directories are not payment/clearing systems.
3. GPU provider and render artifact claims are not independently verified.
4. XRPL and Xaman layers model boundaries but do not prove live finality or wallet approval.
5. Replay/checkpoint/federation records need stronger signature, quorum, and anti-poisoning controls.
6. Creator templates execute JavaScript and need sandboxing/signing before public marketplace use.
7. Gateway/session/player records are not authenticated service records.
8. Script sprawl creates accidental attack surface and confusing public claims.
9. Supply-chain posture is unclear because offline vendor configuration is incomplete in this clone and node_modules exists.
10. Missing open-source policies make vulnerability reporting and acceptable-use boundaries unclear.

Production recommendation
Do not launch public economic, marketplace, wallet, GPU-provider, or player-session claims until non-authoritative scaffolds are clearly separated from authoritative systems and the mitigations in docs/security/abuse-and-adversarial-analysis.md are implemented.
