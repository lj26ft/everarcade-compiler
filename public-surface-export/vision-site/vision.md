# Vision: world.evr

EverArcade exists so communities can build worlds that are portable, verifiable, and persistent.

`world.evr` is the artifact: a world package plus enough evidence for someone else to replay, restore, migrate, operate, and eventually fork or contribute to that world. EverArcade is the ecosystem that makes those artifacts discoverable and useful.

## Why worlds matter

Worlds accumulate history, relationships, assets, governance, and culture. A conventional game server can disappear with that history. A verified world should instead carry its continuity with it.

## The architecture in public terms

- **World Git:** worlds have lineage, contribution opportunities, and fork paths.
- **Verified replay:** independent verifiers can reproduce execution from journals and specs.
- **Restore and migration:** bundles prove a world can be recovered or moved without trusting a stored root as truth.
- **Registry and discovery:** players and operators can find worlds and see proof status.
- **Capability Marketplace:** reusable capabilities can be installed into worlds with visible authorship and verification state.
- **Treasury execution:** world budgets and rewards are governed by explicit policy.
- **Founding Worlds:** the first cohort helps prove the model in public.
